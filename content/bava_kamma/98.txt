Similarly, one who burns a loan document of his fellow so that the loan can't be collected is not liable to pay, for he says to his fellow, "I burned a mere paper of yours." In both cases, the damages are not direct but causative, for which one doesn't pay.<br />
<br />
However, the final law distinguishes between causing the loss directly or indirectly and makes one liable in the above cases.<br />
