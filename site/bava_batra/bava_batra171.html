Bava Batra 171 - Partial Debt Payments without Trust

One who repaid part of his debt will naturally want to ensure that the loan document will not be used against him to collect the debt twice. Unlike in the previous case, they do not have a third party that they both trust. They cannot add to the loan document because an unscrupulous lender can cut this off. Nor can they add to the top of the document because witnesses must sign immediately below the last line.

Rabbi Yehudah says they should write a new loan document for the remainder of the debt, but the borrower should not be forced to guard the receipt. Rabbi Yossi says they should write a receipt, and the borrower should guard it since people pay more significant debts faster and since "a borrower is a servant to the lender." The accepted law follows Rabbi Yossi.
