Bava Batra 85 - One's Container Formalizes Acquisition

Rav and Shmuel both said, "A person's container can formalize acquisition for him in any place - except in the public domain." Rabbi Yochanan and Resh Lakish both said, "Even in the public domain."

A person's container can act as his courtyard to acquire items placed in it, provided that the container is placed in a domain where the owner has the right to place it. Thus, a buyer can acquire items in the seller's domain by placing them in his container, provided that the seller gives him the right to place the container there.
